Understanding Import Parity Gap

The Import Parity Gap represents the difference between the landed cost of imported metal (International COMEX price converted to INR) and the domestic trading price on MCX.

Import Parity (₹/g) = (International Price × USDINR Rate) / 28.3

What it signals:

  • Positive Premium (Green): Domestic prices are higher than international rates. This usually indicates strong local demand, supply constraints, or high import duties.
  • Negative Discount (Red): Domestic prices are lower than international rates. This can happen during weak local demand or significant inventory liquidations locally.
Data sourced from Yahoo Finance & TradingView · INR/g derived using standard conversion factor (28.3g/oz)
